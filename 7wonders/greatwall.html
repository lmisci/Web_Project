The Great Wall of China

Between the 18th and 20th centuries, the Great Wall became the most common symbol of China for the Western world, and a symbol both physical–a manifestation of Chinese strength–and psychological–a representation of the blockade maintained by the Chinese state to block out foreign influences and exercise control over its citizens.
